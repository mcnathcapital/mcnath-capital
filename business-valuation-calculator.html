What's the difference between revenue multiples and EBITDA multiples?

Revenue multiples are simpler but less precise - good for early estimates. EBITDA multiples are more accurate because they account for profitability differences. Most professional buyers use EBITDA multiples, while revenue multiples are common for high-growth or asset-light businesses.

When do I need a professional business valuation?

Professional appraisals ($2,500-$15,000) are required for: business sales, SBA loan applications over $500K, estate/gift tax filings, divorce proceedings, partnership disputes, and ESOP transactions. Our calculator is perfect for initial planning, negotiations preparation, and regular value tracking.

How can I increase my business value?

The highest-impact improvements: 1) Convert customers to recurring contracts (+20-30%), 2) Reduce owner dependency through management hiring (+15-25%), 3) Diversify customer base - no client >20% revenue (+10-15%), 4) Document all processes and systems (+8-12%), 5) Improve profit margins through operational efficiency (+variable based on improvement).

What's driving business valuations in 2026?

Key trends: Strategic buyers paying premium multiples for market consolidation, private equity has record $3.7T in dry powder seeking deals, baby boomers creating supply of businesses for sale, and technology integration requirements driving "buy vs. build" decisions. How long does it take to sell a business?

Typical timeline: Preparation (3-6 months): Financial cleanup, management strengthening, process documentation. Marketing (4-8 months): Buyer identification, negotiations, due diligence. Closing (30-60 days): Legal documentation and final approvals. Total: 8-14 months for optimal results.
